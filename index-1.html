01 · Overview

Executive Summary

A high-level view of what we are building, the business it runs, and the value it delivers to a peptide brokerage operating B2B into pharmacies and clinic owners.

This proposal outlines the design, feasibility, and delivery plan for Agent OS — a white-labeled, multi-agent AI platform that serves as the operational backbone of your peptide brokerage. Rather than bolting individual chatbots or dialers onto existing tools, Agent OS is a single, coherent system in which specialized AI agents — Sales SDR, Inbound Sales, Customer Support, Appointment Setter, Account Manager, and Quote & Order — collaborate through a central orchestrator that routes conversations, shares memory, and enforces compliance.

Every buyer interaction — whether an inbound inquiry from a pharmacy owner, an outbound sequence to a medspa decision-maker, a support ticket on a pending shipment, or a follow-up two weeks after an unclosed quote — is handled by an agent with the right tools, the right tone, and the right context. The platform is white-labeled end-to-end: your branding on the admin console, on outbound communications, and on the customer-facing widgets. It is multi-tenant at the foundation, so the same platform can later be productized and licensed to peer brokers as a SaaS business.

Because peptides sit in a regulated pharmaceutical category, Agent OS is designed with a dedicated Compliance & Governance Layer — business license verification of buyers, guardrails that prevent off-label or medical claims, consent-aware call recording, full audit trails, and region-aware outreach rules (TCPA, CAN-SPAM, GDPR).

03 · Features

Platform Modules

The platform is organized into six functional modules that together form a coherent operating system — not a collection of tools.

Module A

Multi-Agent Orchestration Engine

Agent Router & Handoff

Every inbound message or event is classified and routed to the correct agent in milliseconds. Mid-conversation handoffs preserve full context — no "let me transfer you" reset.

  • Intent-based routing (LLM classifier)
  • Context-preserving handoffs
  • Fallback rules when an agent is uncertain
  • Human escalation with full transcript

Shared Memory Layer

All agents read from and write to a single, structured memory tied to the buyer account — long-term preferences, short-term conversation state, and cross-channel history.

  • Per-account long-term memory
  • Cross-channel conversation stitching
  • Structured facts + unstructured summaries
  • GDPR-compliant forget & export
Module B

Multi-Channel Communication

Voice Channel

Sub-second-latency inbound and outbound calling with natural-sounding voices. DID numbers, IVR, and live transfer to your team when an agent flags escalation.

Email & Sequences

Inbox integration with deliverability best practices — SPF/DKIM/DMARC, domain warmup, sending-rate control, and full thread-level attribution.

SMS, WhatsApp & Chat

Twilio + WhatsApp Business API + embeddable web chat widget. Same agents, same memory, different surface.

Module C

CRM & Pipeline Management

Accounts & Contacts

Purpose-built CRM for B2B pharma: pharmacy or clinic as the primary account, decision-maker contacts, license docs, shipping addresses, and payment terms all on one record.

  • Pharmacy / clinic account hierarchy
  • Decision-maker & stakeholder mapping
  • License & document vault
  • Account health scoring

Pipeline & Deal Flow

Visual Kanban pipelines with stages you configure. Agents automatically move deals forward based on detected events — quote sent, quote accepted, payment received, reorder due.

  • Configurable pipeline stages
  • Event-driven stage progression
  • Forecast & revenue reporting
  • Export to HubSpot / Salesforce if needed
Module D

Compliance & Governance

Buyer Verification

Before any quote is issued, the platform verifies the buyer is a legitimate licensed pharmacy or clinic — via NPI lookup, state licensing boards, or your preferred verification provider.

  • NPI registry lookup (US)
  • State pharmacy board verification
  • Business registration verification
  • Document collection & expiry tracking

Message & Claim Guardrails

Every outbound message passes through the Compliance Guardrail Agent. It blocks medical-advice phrasing, off-label claims, and anything outside the approved message library.

  • Claim library with approved language
  • Pre-send inspection & rewrite
  • Immutable audit log of all messages
  • Regulator-ready export format
Module E

White-Label Admin Console

Branded Operator Dashboard

Your logo, your colors, your domain. An operator dashboard for your team to monitor conversations, override agents, approve flagged messages, and review performance in real time.

  • Live conversation stream
  • Take-over / whisper mode
  • Approval queue for flagged items
  • Role-based access control

Multi-Tenant Ready

The architecture supports isolated tenants out of the box — so you can later offer the platform as a branded SaaS to peer brokers without rebuilding anything.

  • Tenant-isolated data & memory
  • Per-tenant branding & config
  • Per-tenant billing & usage metering
  • Reseller-ready admin hierarchy
Module F

Analytics & Performance

Conversation Intelligence

Every conversation is auto-summarized, tagged, sentiment-scored, and indexed. You can ask the platform "what are buyers asking about most this week?" in plain English.

Funnel & Revenue Reporting

Attribution from first touch to paid order, cohort-level retention, agent-level performance, and channel ROI — all exportable.

05 · Compliance

Pharmaceutical Guardrails

Peptides sit in a regulated pharmaceutical category. This section maps the specific risks to the specific mitigations the platform ships with — because a generic AI sales stack is not safe to point at pharmacy and clinic buyers.

Risk / ConcernWhere it shows upHow Agent OS handles itPosture
Selling to unlicensed buyers Inbound inquiries from non-pharmacy contacts (gym owners, individuals) Hard block at qualification: Inbound Sales Agent + Compliance Guardrail require verified business license or NPI before any quote is generated. Very High
Off-label / medical claims by an agent A sales agent extrapolates therapeutic benefits to close a deal Every outbound message passes the Compliance Guardrail, which matches against an approved claim library. Anything not in the library is blocked and routed to human review. Very High
TCPA / CAN-SPAM / GDPR violations Cold outbound calls, SMS, email Channel-level consent tracking. DNC list enforcement. Quiet-hours respect per timezone. Unsubscribe honored across all channels & agents. Very High
Call recording without consent Voice channel, two-party-consent jurisdictions Automatic jurisdiction detection + consent preamble on all recorded calls. Recording is off by default in strict jurisdictions. High
Handling of patient data A clinic buyer accidentally shares PHI in a message PHI detection in the guardrail pipeline redacts & flags. Platform is not marketed as HIPAA-BAA-covered; legal review recommended if PHI flow is expected. Moderate
Controlled-substance peptides Certain peptides may be scheduled or restricted in specific states/countries Catalog-level restriction flags. Geo-aware availability: the quote engine refuses to price a restricted SKU into a jurisdiction that prohibits it. High
Full audit trail for regulators Any future inquiry into what was said, to whom, when Append-only event log of every message, decision, tool call, and guardrail verdict. Exportable to regulator-friendly formats. Very High

Honest note on jurisdiction. Peptide regulation varies significantly by country and by U.S. state — some peptides are FDA-approved prescription drugs, some are research-chemicals-only, some are restricted outright. Before go-live we will run a jurisdictional scoping workshop with your counsel to lock down: (1) which SKUs you are authorized to broker, (2) which buyer types qualify, (3) which regions you will and won't serve, and (4) the approved claim library. The platform enforces whatever rules you set — but the rules themselves need your legal team's sign-off, not ours.
06 · Feasibility

Feasibility Analysis

Structured assessment of technical, operational, legal, and commercial feasibility.

⚙️ Technical Feasibility

Very High

Every building block is production-proven. Multi-agent orchestration frameworks (LangGraph, custom Claude-based graphs), low-latency voice stacks (Vapi, Retell, ElevenLabs Conversational AI), email/SMS infrastructure (Twilio, Postmark, SendGrid), and vector memory (pgvector, Pinecone) are all mature. This is engineering, not research.

⚖️ Regulatory Feasibility

Review Needed

The platform itself is regulator-neutral — it enforces whatever rules you configure. What needs legal review is the business model: which peptides, which jurisdictions, which buyer types. We recommend a jurisdictional scoping workshop with your counsel in Week 1 before any claims or catalog go live.

🏪 Operational Feasibility

High

Day-to-day running requires a small operator team (1–3 people) to monitor the approval queue, handle escalations, and update the catalog. The admin console is built for non-technical staff. Onboarding a new buyer account takes minutes, not hours.

📈 Scalability Feasibility

Very High

Horizontally scalable by design — containerized services, stateless agents, queue-backed channel workers. The same platform handles 10 conversations a day or 10,000. Multi-tenant foundations mean zero rework to productize for resale.

🎙️ Voice Agent Quality

Conditional

Voice agents in 2026 are genuinely good — natural latency, interruption handling, realistic prosody. That said, the very first call a buyer gets should sound polished. We budget a dedicated voice-tuning phase (Phase 4) for prompt crafting, voice selection, and objection-handling rehearsal against real sales scripts before voice goes live.

💰 Commercial Feasibility

Strong ROI

Two stacked returns: (1) labor substitution — a small team running SDR, support, and appointment setting work that would otherwise require 5–8 hires; (2) optionality — because the platform is multi-tenant and white-labeled from day one, you can license it to peer brokers as a SaaS, creating a recurring revenue line on top of the core business.

🔌 Integration Feasibility

High

All required integrations — Google/Outlook calendars, Stripe, Twilio, WhatsApp Business, email infrastructure, NPI lookup, US state pharmacy boards — have stable, documented APIs. If you have an existing ERP or accounting system, a lightweight connector is straightforward.

🧩 Vendor Lock-in Risk

Low

The orchestrator is model-agnostic; agents can be moved between Claude, GPT, or open-weights models with config changes. Voice providers (Vapi, Retell, Twilio direct) are swappable behind the channel layer. Data is yours, in your Postgres, exportable anytime.

08 · Risks

Risk Assessment

The risks that actually matter for a project of this shape, and the mitigation we've designed in.

Agent Hallucination on Clinical Questions

High

An agent improvising an answer about peptide dosing, contraindications, or therapeutic effect — inappropriate for a broker to speak on, and potentially a regulatory incident.

✓ Mitigation. Compliance Guardrail with an approved claim library. Hard refusal template for all clinical questions with routing to a human expert or pharmacist. This is non-optional and ships in Phase 2.

Buyer Misrepresentation

Medium

A non-licensed buyer poses as a clinic or pharmacy to obtain product.

✓ Mitigation. Multi-source license verification (NPI + state board + business registration). Document collection with expiry tracking. High-value orders additionally require human approval regardless of agent verdict.

Voice Latency & Awkwardness

Medium

A voice agent that stumbles, interrupts the buyer, or has audible "thinking" pauses damages brand perception — especially for a first-contact call.

✓ Mitigation. Dedicated voice-tuning phase (Phase 4). Streaming speech-to-text, predictive response generation, natural filler phrases, interruption handling. Voice goes live only after passing a script-based acceptance test.

Regulatory Shift in Peptides

Medium

Regulation of specific peptides or peptide classes could tighten, making a previously-allowed SKU restricted or requiring new claim language.

✓ Mitigation. Catalog-level restriction flags and a central claim library — both editable by your compliance lead without engineering involvement. A policy update takes minutes, not a release cycle.

Prompt Injection & Jailbreaks

Medium

A hostile actor crafts messages designed to make an agent bypass its compliance rules.

✓ Mitigation. The Compliance Guardrail is post-hoc and out-of-band — it reviews every outbound message regardless of what the agent was instructed. Attempts to manipulate are logged and rate-limited.

Scope Creep

Low

"Can we also have an invoicing agent? A procurement agent? A competitor intel agent?" — each small addition compounds timeline and complexity.

✓ Mitigation. Signed SRS at end of Phase 1 defining the exact eight agents and their scope. Additions handled via formal change request with updated timeline and cost.

Model Provider Dependency

Low

A single model provider has an outage or raises prices substantially.

✓ Mitigation. Model-agnostic Layer 3. Every agent has a primary and fallback model configured. Outage of one provider degrades gracefully instead of hard-failing.

Deliverability Collapse on Outbound Email

Low

Aggressive outbound sequences land in spam, destroying sender reputation on your primary domain.

✓ Mitigation. Separate outbound domains with proper warmup, strict SPF/DKIM/DMARC, conservative daily limits per inbox, and real-time engagement-based throttling. The primary corporate domain is never used for cold outbound.
